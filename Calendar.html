Fish Migration

January-March
At this time period, the fish moves to the deaper side if the lake away from the show since there is no fresh water joing the lake that comes with fresh food. Some species like the Nile pearch and Tilapia are mostly found past Ringiti island, past Remba and somehow around Migingo island. At this time period Mfangano, Rusinga and area towards Kisumu area produce good quantity of 'Omena' and 'Fulu'. The omena specie is mostly cought at a high quantity during the dark periods when the moon is not shining.
March-May
During this time period fish migrate from the inner side of the lake to the show because of the fresh water that is increased by the rainy season. At this time most of the fish species are found next to the fish shows except Nile perch and Tilapia though they move abit closer.
June-September
At this time Fish catch is less since the mostly had a great catch during the march- may period so the remaining are moslty fingerlets of small sizes which are recomended to be fished. At this time the fishermen are urged to take a break for a month and allow the fingerlets grow to a recomended size.
October-December
At this time slot the fihsermen are free toget back for fishing and they should go deeper due to less rain in the lake region, therefore some species of fish go deeper in fetch of food and some small species like remain around the show.

Mfangano Island

This is an island popular for producing omena, Tilapia and Nile-perch, the island also provide a good envirnment for agriculture more so fish farming, growing farm produce and even rearing animals. It is located on the west of Rusinga and immedialtly before Ringiti island. The avarage depth around this isalnd is 41M. It adviced that the fishermen should be keen during rainy season when they travel or use boats around this area since the waves seems bigger. At some areas of the shore are bushy and are prone to hippopotomus and crocodile attacks.


Migingo

This island is approximatly 2000 square-metre large and located to the south of Remba island well known for omena, Tilapia and 'Fulu'. It cose to the Kenya-Uganda border and our fishermen should be keen always not to cross over the Uganda to avoid conflicts with the Ugandan government. This area is averagely 63M of depth. This area is free from crocodile and hippopotomus attack but prone to huge waves during the rainy season. It produces good quantity of fish and also good frofish farming.


MuhuruBay

Muhuru bay is a beach in migori county known for Tilapia and omena, it has a good environment for fishing with predictable weather condtions with somehow small waves as comapared to the islands but also with hinger rate of crocodile and hippopotomus attacks. The fishermen and those who live around this area should avoid the bushy areas where the crocodiles and hippos find their habitat.


Ringiti

This is an island located immediately after Mfangano Island, it also deep around it show with ann average depth of 50M. It is close to the Kenya-Uganda border, free from animal attacks, good for rearing pigs, provides good environment for fish farming but with high and heavy waves during harsh weather conditions. It carries population of about 3282 people as per the last census. It majorly produces omena, 'Fulu', Tilapia, Nile perch and other types of fish.


Rusinga-Mbita

Rusinga is an island connected to mbita by a courseway, it located to the East of Mfangano and it is known to produce a variety of fish species at averagely good quantity. It carries a population of 26,351 people with an area of 44 square-kilometre. it provides a good condition for fish rearing with relatively low and weaker waves as compared the other highlands.
